Competitions and Awards

Some of my projects that brought home a trophy.

Adobe Kickbox Innovation Competiton

This was a pretty dope experience because it was separate from the company I was with. It's kind of a paint by numbers development life cycle experience in just one workshop. The Kickbox was rebranded Red Box Crusher but the materials and intent were all the same. Kickbox aims for two goals: creating new businesses while playfully educating your employees and making them fit for the digital age.

Enterprise Fitness App Health & Wellness

After the Red Box workshop and doing all of the steps for that you could take the next challenge which was to take an idea and make it a reality. The firm I was with would give you $1K in cash to use toward the build. Knowing their target audience, they add the stipulation that couldn't use it on food or booze, but everything else was game. So, I spent my money on a bunch of Fitbit watches and scales. Using the Fitbit API and corporate data I created a firm wide wellness app.

Hackapalooza Innovation Competiton

This was a recurring challenge I was a part of. The great thing about this was my company would give you a week off your main gig to do this. So, you could just burn through your side of the desk projects in one week. Getting time to just make things and make your company better was amazing. I probably did 4 projects through this that all had significant impacts on the business.

Improvaganza Application competition

My favorite competition, honestly. You could take some piece of technology that has been sitting around for years and give it some love. On this one I took all our brick & mortar locations and built an information website containing all of the details on them. This had previously been maintained by 3 or 4 Excel workbooks at various states of being out of date.

Inventor Gala Patent Holder

I'm just bragging at this point. On one of the first ever Hackathons I was a part of, myself and a team of stockbrokers built a training website on how to trade options. The idea behind the trading training displays has been working its way through the US Patent process. The company I was with put together a cool Inventor Gala at the Fort Museum in Ann Arbor for patent holders. It was a lot of fun and my first Gala, so it bears mention.
